Who is Yolanda?
Yolanda is my second daughter.
newborn
She was born on August 2018. I was standing by the obstetric delivery bed when she was born. At that time, I didn't fully understand the significance of the black birthmarks on her body. The picture on the left was taken on the day she was born. Despite being asleep, she had her fists clenched and her arms raised, as if ready to fight. Neither she nor I knew that she would have to battle MAS for the rest of her life.
4 months old
When Yolanda was 4 months old, her skin was allergic to a body cream and we took her to see a doctor. The doctor noticed the black birthmarks on her body and mentioned that they were not a good sign, but couldn't provide further details. So, we remained unaware of what lay ahead until Yolanda turned 6 months old.
6 months old
When Yolanda was 6 months old, my wife noticed blood in her diaper while changing it. We got panic and took her to the hospital. After consulting multiple doctors and undergoing thorough examinations, we received the heart-wrenching news that she had MAS. Despite being in the best child hospital in the province, there was no cure or treatment available since MAS is a rare disease, and the doctors lacked experience in dealing with it.
2 years old
Time passed, and apart from the black birthmarks, Yolanda grew up just like any other kid and turned 2 years old. The picture on the right was taken when she was happily playing with her elder sister one day before she broke her left thigh (Yolanda is on the right). The next day, while running happily from the bedroom to the living room, she fell to the ground and cried out aloud. As soon as I heard her cry, I knew the injury must be serious. Because despite being my younger daughter, Yolanda had always been the tough-minded one in the family and rarely cry over little injuries. The moment I picked her up from the ground, I knew immediately that her bones must have been broken. We took her to the hospital but the doctors told us that her bones were hollowed, similar to tofu. And they couldn't perform surgery as it could lead to further fractures. Yolanda spent two weeks in the hospital with only skin traction before being discharged. She continued lying in bed at home for a few more weeks.
Fortunately, Yolanda recovered from the incident and was able to walk later. However, due to the bone fracture, her left and right thighbones are not of equal length, resulting in a slight limp. Yolanda was too young to fully comprehend the situation, and she remained happy as usual. The picture on the left was taken in February 2021 while she was playing with her elder sister (Yolanda is on the right).
After returning from the hospital, I started searching for more information about MAS and see what else we could do for Yolanda. Fortunately I found some support groups for MAS patients and connected with other parents. We shared information about doctors and hospitals specializing in MAS treatment. It was through this group that I learned about a doctor in a hospital 300 km away who had experience treating MAS patients. In July 2021, I took Yolanda to see this doctor for the first time and injected bisphosphonates for her to increase her bone density. At first the injection was scheduled every 6 months, and later changed to every 3 months.
5 years old
Time flew by. Despite Yolanda's slight limp, life remained calm and peaceful. On November 19, 2023, Yolanda turned 5 years old and history repeated itself. Although it was winter and she wore thick clothes, it did not help at all. While playing at home, Yolanda fell and broke her left thigh once again. This time, it was not entirely unexpected, as routine X-rays had shown the deformity in her left collum femoris (upper end of her femur) and I had the feeling that it might cause problem one day. We rushed her to the hospital, faced the same dilemma of whether to opt for surgery or not. Luckily this time, after hearing about my daughter's situation, a patient's father in the chat group recommended his son's doctor to me. It was also a coincidence that the doctor just transferred his work to a hospital near us (from a hospital 1000 km away to a hospital 200 km away) two months before. So we transferred Yolanda to this hospital, where she underwent surgery on Nov 27th and an intramedullary lengthening nail were used to join and strengthen her left thighbone. The picture on the right shows her left thighbone after the surgery. Yolanda was discharged on Dec 2nd and rested on bed until March 2024.
now
She has made good progress in her recovery and is now able to walk. During her recent hospital visit, the medication used for injections was updated as the previous medication was no longer effective in supporting her body. Continuing to use the old medication may result in the protrusion of the nail implant.
